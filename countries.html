FTL Scene Radar

European lacrosse is not one scene — it is thirty different stories at thirty different speeds. Here is how we read the momentum right now.

Hot Right Now
🏴 England

European Box Champions in 2022. Fourth at the 2024 World Championship. England’s Box programme is setting the continental benchmark and their domestic scene keeps producing internationally competitive players.

Explore England →
Building Quietly
🇫🇷 France

From zero clubs in 2006 to 12 in 2025. A championship relaunched. A Héritage team for the diaspora. France is assembling infrastructure without headlines — which is exactly how lasting scenes are built.

FieldSixesBox
Explore France →
Sixes Momentum
🇳🇱 Netherlands

EuroLax Sixes Cup winners. ELF Championship Silver in 2008. NLB was building Sixes infrastructure before the Olympic announcement made it a continental priority. Watch the Dutch in the next qualification cycle.

SixesField
Explore Netherlands →
Box Stronghold
🇨🇿 Czech Rep.

Prague hosts the Aleš Hřebeský Memorial every year — the only European Box tournament that draws North American club teams. The Czech scene does not need validation from the outside. It is the outside that comes to Prague.

BoxField
Explore Czech Republic →
Champions Pedigree
🇩🇪 Germany

ELF founding member (1995). ELF Championship Gold in 2001 — beating England 16-8 in the final. Three Silver medals (1999, 2000, 2004). DLaxV has been building a full lacrosse infrastructure since the early 1990s, Field and Box both.

FieldBox
Explore Germany →
Worth Watching
🇪🇸 Spain

Barcelona is building something real. Sixes-first, young, and strategically aligned with the 2028 Olympic cycle. Spain is not yet a top-10 European scene — but it is moving faster than most people have noticed.

SixesField
Explore Spain →
ELF Finalist
🇮🇹 Italy

ELF Championship Silver in 2025 — lost 9-8 to Israel in the final. 8 active clubs. A structured youth pathway. FIGL has been building quietly since 2007 and the results are starting to show.

FieldSixes
Explore Italy →
Founding Depth
🇸🇪 Sweden

ELF founding member since 1995. Thirty years of consistent national participation, university-rooted, now aligning a Sixes programme with the Olympic cycle. Quiet consistency is its own kind of power.

FieldSixes
Explore Sweden →

Right now · France 🇫🇷

The 2026 ELF Championship Test

France has been building quietly for 20 years. The 2026 ELF Championship is the first real stress test. The October 2025 win over England was a data point — is it a trend?

Olympic angle · Netherlands 🇳🇱 + Spain 🇪🇸

The Sixes Window Before 2028

The scenes that move on Sixes in the next 18 months will define European lacrosse for a decade after 2028. Netherlands and Spain are best positioned. NLB won the EuroLax Sixes Cup. Spain launched AEL in 2010. Watch both.

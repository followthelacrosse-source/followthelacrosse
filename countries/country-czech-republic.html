01 Overview

The Czech Republic Lacrosse Scene

Lacrosse arrived in Czech Republic in 1986. By the time the ELF was founded in 1995, Czech Republic was already a credible national programme — not just a founding member on paper, but a competitive force. They hosted the inaugural ELF Championship in 1995 and finished as Silver medallist, then repeated that result in 1997.

The Aleš Hřebeský Memorial, first held in 1994 in memory of a founding figure of Czech lacrosse, grew into the continent's defining Box lacrosse event — the only European tournament that draws North American club teams consistently. By its 32nd edition in 2025, the AHM hosted 24 teams from 19 countries.

The Czech Lacrosse Union (CLU) was formally established on 12 May 2004, institutionalising a scene that had already been competing internationally for nearly two decades.

— Key Milestones

1986

Lacrosse Introduced

Lacrosse takes root in Czech Republic. By 1986 the game has an early competitive presence that will grow into one of Europe's strongest Box cultures.

1994

First Aleš Hřebeský Memorial

The AHM is created in memory of Aleš Hřebeský, who died on 30 November 1993. The first edition is held in Prague the following year — the tournament that will become Europe's most internationally significant Box event.

1995 · 1997

ELF Founding & Silver Medals

Czech Republic co-founds the European Lacrosse Federation in 1995 and hosts the inaugural ELF Championship, finishing as Silver medallist. Silver again in 1997.

2004

CLU Formally Established

The Czech Lacrosse Union is officially founded on 12 May 2004, providing the institutional structure for a scene that had been competing internationally for nearly 20 years.

01 Overview

Lacrosse in England

England has the longest lacrosse history of any European nation. The sport arrived via First Nations exhibitions at Crystal Palace in 1867, followed by Canadian touring sides in 1876 and 1883. By that point, England already had 60 clubs playing in Cambridgeshire, Cheshire, Lancashire, Middlesex, and Yorkshire.

The English Lacrosse Union — the first national governing body for the men’s game in Europe — was formed in 1892. Women’s lacrosse followed, with the Ladies’ Lacrosse Association founded in 1912. The two organisations merged in 1996 and rebranded as England Lacrosse in 2019.

Today, England is the most successful European club nation in the Ken Galluccio Cup (European club championship), having won 8 of the 11 editions. Greater Manchester hosted the World Lacrosse Championship three times: 1978, 1994, and 2010.

02 Structure

Clubs & Leagues

Club lacrosse in England is divided into northern and southern leagues, each with their own Premiership and multiple divisions. The season typically runs from September to April.

NEMLA

North of England Men’s Lacrosse Association, founded 1897. Covers northern England with a Premiership and four further senior divisions.

SEMLA

South of England Men’s Lacrosse Association, formed 1882. Covers southern England and Wales, with Premier Division, Division 1 and Division 2.

04 Why Follow

Why Follow England Lacrosse?

🏆

The most successful European club scene

English clubs have won 8 of the 11 Ken Galluccio Cup editions. If you want to understand what European club lacrosse excellence looks like, start here.

🏠

Box lacrosse done right

England’s Box programme is the benchmark for European indoor lacrosse. European champions in 2022, 4th at the 2024 World Championship — and a domestic Box structure that other nations are trying to copy.

📖

150 years of institutional memory

No other European nation has been playing lacrosse longer. That history shapes the culture, the rivalries, and the infrastructure — and makes England a reference point for understanding where European lacrosse comes from.

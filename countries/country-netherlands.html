01 Overview

The Netherlands Lacrosse Scene

The Netherlands occupies one of the most distinctive positions in European lacrosse. While most nations built around Field, the Dutch embraced Sixes early — and when World Lacrosse chose Sixes as the Olympic format, the Netherlands was already ahead of the curve.

NLB (Nederlandse Lacrosse Bond) has governed Dutch lacrosse since 2003. The men's national team claimed Silver at the 2008 ELF Championship, and the Dutch won the EuroLax Sixes Cup in overtime — one of the most significant Sixes results in European history.

The club scene is centred around Amsterdam and extends to Rotterdam, The Hague, and Utrecht. Field and Sixes run alongside each other in the NLB structure.

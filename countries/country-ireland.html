01 Overview

The Ireland Lacrosse Scene

Ireland Lacrosse sits among Europe's strongest growth nations. Affiliated to the European Lacrosse Federation since 2002, the federation has built an integrated domestic pipeline across field, box and sixes — rare for a mid-sized European scene.

The senior men's team qualified for the 2027 World Championship via the 2025 European Championship in Wrocław, with a decisive quarter-final win over Germany. One year earlier, the U20 men took European gold by defeating England 10-7, signalling a generation of Irish players ready to convert age-group success into senior results.

The domestic structure is anchored by the Irish Lacrosse League (ILL), the women's WILL and the National Indoor NILL. Women's box had its inaugural edition in 2024 and women's sixes debuted in 2025, with men's sixes scheduled for 2026.

03 National Teams

Ireland on the World Stage

Senior · 2027 WC qualifier

Men's National Team

Senior men qualified for the 2027 World Championship at the 2025 Euros in Wrocław, with a quarter-final win over Germany. The U20 side took European gold in 2024 by defeating England 10-7.

Women · Multi-format

Women's Programme

Women's box launched with an inaugural edition in 2024 and women's sixes made its debut in 2025. Team Éire Green finished runners-up at the 32-team Heritage Cup 2025.

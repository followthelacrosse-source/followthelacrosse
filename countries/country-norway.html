01 Overview

The Norway Lacrosse Scene

Norway joined the European Lacrosse Federation in 2008, and its lacrosse scene has been built substantially through the university system. NHH Vikings in Bergen (founded 2004) and BI-Lions in Oslo (founded 2005) were the pioneering clubs, establishing the university-club model that gave Norwegian lacrosse its early structure and competitive depth.

Today, Norway has 16 active clubs and approximately 1,000 registered players nationwide — numbers that make it one of the larger lacrosse nations in Scandinavia. Within that network, the Oslo Legends stand out: an 8-time national champion club that has defined the domestic competitive ceiling. The Norwegian national team is coached by Dan Scolaro.

At the 2025 European Championship, Norway secured a narrow win over Portugal that qualified the men’s national team for Division II of the 2027 World Championship — a significant milestone for a programme that has grown methodically from university roots into a genuine competitive force in Northern European lacrosse.

Lacrosse in Denmark

Danish lacrosse began in 2002, started by one enthusiast who had seen the sport in Canada. Twenty years later, Dansk Lacrosse Forbund is rebuilding — founding-ELF member, three domestic clubs, a 2025 European Championship on the record.

01 Overview

The Danish Lacrosse Scene

Danish lacrosse has one of the most human founding stories in European lacrosse. Kristian Gotsch saw the sport in Canada, came home in 2002, and started Dansk Lacrosse Forbund (DLF) effectively from nothing. No existing structure, no imported league — just one founder and a Danish federation that would go on to become an ELF founding member nation.

The arc that followed is honest, not heroic. The scene grew between 2003 and 2009, hit a crisis between 2010 and 2015 where the whole programme nearly collapsed, and began a slow recovery from around 2014 onward. In 2015, the Copenhagen Lacrosse Cup hosted nine men's and five women's international teams — the clearest sign that the Danish hub was alive again.

In 2016 DLF hired Nick Ravenhall, a former captain of New Zealand at the 2014 World Games, to rebuild the men's programme. The recovery is still in progress: at the 2025 European Championship in Wrocław, Denmark lost 14–4 to France in the group stage, with goalkeeper David Gudman back in the national team lineup.

Denmark is not a dominant European side. It does something rarer: it demonstrates that a small Nordic federation can survive a near-collapse and still compete on the continent two decades after being started by a single person.

02 Structure

Clubs & Structure

Denmark on the World Stage

Field · 2025 Euros

Men's National Team

Head coach Nick Ravenhall (2016–), former New Zealand captain at the 2014 World Games. At the 2025 European Championship in Wrocław, Denmark lost 14–4 to France in the group stage, with goalkeeper David Gudman on the roster.

Women · DLF pathway

Women's Programme

Denmark's women's pathway has historical roots back to the Copenhagen Lacrosse Cup 2015, which already hosted five women's international teams. DLF continues women's development alongside the men's rebuild.

04 Why Follow

Why Follow Denmark?

🔔

A founding ELF nation

Denmark is one of the original members of the European Lacrosse Federation (1995). That status is historically important for reading how modern European lacrosse was built — and by whom.

💪

Resilience, not dominance

The Danish arc — growth, near-collapse, recovery — is rare in European sport records. A federation that survived its own crisis is a federation worth watching differently than one that simply accumulates medals.

🌍

Founded by one person

Kristian Gotsch came back from Canada in 2002 and built a national federation from scratch. The story is instructive for anyone watching how European lacrosse scenes actually start.

🏃

Back on the European floor

The 2025 Euros were not a winning tournament for Denmark, but showing up at all — with Nick Ravenhall still coaching and David Gudman in goal — is the story. The recovery is a live story.

01 Overview

The Swiss Lacrosse Scene

Switzerland occupies a distinctive position in European lacrosse. Where many nations built around a single format, Swisslax has run field, box and sixes side by side since its first years — a multi-format approach that still feels rare on the continent.

The federation, Swiss Lacrosse Federation (Swisslax), was founded in 2006 and admitted as a full member of World Lacrosse in 2008. Twenty years on, the network has grown to 13 member clubs for the 2025–26 season, including new entrant Lugano Falcons, which extends the Swisslax footprint into Italian-speaking Ticino for the first time.

The 2025–26 cycle has been a visibility year. The Swiss Sixes championship final took place in Chur on 8 November 2025, and Radiotelevisione svizzera (RSI) featured lacrosse as an emerging Swiss sport in September 2025 — a first-time exposure on national public broadcasting.

In parallel, Swisslax is working toward recognition by the Swiss Olympic Committee. That track matters: combined with Sixes being the LA28 lacrosse format, it positions Switzerland as a federation moving from sport association to institutional actor.

03 National Teams

Switzerland on the World Stage

Field · Box · Sixes

Men's National Teams

Swiss men's sides compete across all three World Lacrosse disciplines. The multi-format Swisslax pathway is rare in European lacrosse and remains one of the federation's defining strengths.

Women's Programme

Colleen Niklaus was appointed head coach of the Swiss women's programme in January 2026, opening a new cycle for the national team as Sixes development continues across Swisslax clubs.

04 Why Follow

Why Follow Switzerland?

🏗️

Multi-format pioneer

Field, box and sixes run in parallel inside the same federation. Few European nations operate all three disciplines at national-championship level — Switzerland does, and has done since its early years.

📱

First national TV crossover

Radiotelevisione svizzera (RSI) featured Swiss lacrosse as an emerging sport in September 2025. That kind of public-service exposure is a marker that lacrosse has crossed from closed circuit to visible Swiss sport.

🏍

Institutional trajectory

Swisslax is actively pursuing Swiss Olympic Committee recognition. In an Olympic cycle where Sixes is the LA28 format, that institutional work is where long-term federation credibility is built.

🏞️

Ticino expansion

Lugano Falcons joined Swisslax for 2025–26, extending the Swiss network into the Italian-speaking canton for the first time. A small move, but the kind that signals a federation still growing laterally.
